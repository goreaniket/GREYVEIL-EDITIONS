The Human Fiction

We do not live in reality. We live in stories.

Back to CollectionContact Greyveil

Nations are stories. Religions are stories. Money is a story. Identity is a story. Even the person staring back from the mirror is a story continuously rewritten by memory.

Human beings are the only creatures capable of imagining things that do not exist and then acting as if they do.

Civilizations were built from fiction. Wars were fought for fiction. Love survives through fiction. Meaning itself is fiction.

The Human Fiction explores the invisible narratives that shape human existence—the stories inherited, invented, remembered and believed.

Through psychological narratives and philosophical fiction, this collection journeys into the fragile architecture of identity, memory, truth and imagination.

Because what we call reality may simply be the strongest story ever told.

Inside the Series

And perhaps the greatest fiction of all is the self.
The name you answer to.
The memories you protect.
The beliefs you defend.
The person you think you are.

A narrative assembled over time and mistaken for something permanent.

The Human Mind asked what consciousness is.
The Human Paradox asked why we are divided.
The Human Fiction asks a far more unsettling question:

What if the person reading these words is also a story?
